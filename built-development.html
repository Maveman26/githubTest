Built Environment

The way we move people and goods is changing - whether on foot, wheels, rails or in the air. How do we provide access

to mobility in ways that are efficient, equitable, sustainable and affordable? With some of the highest capital needs of service

areas, how can cities, counties, agencies and regions ensure decisions are made strategically, effectively and collaboratively?

Here are some of the solutions and momentum underway throughout North Texas.

 

Built Environment

Whether pavement, pipes, placemaking or streetlights, the built environment impacts every day of our lives. Cities are taking a

fresh look at how to design, permit, maintain and build new and existing infrastructure using new materials and tools. In this -

greater process, time and financial efficiencies can be gained to put resources into additional services.

Dallas - Smart Cities Living Lab: The Dallas Innovation Alliance (DIA) created and implemented its

Smart Cities Living Lab in the West End of Downtown Dallas, as part of the burgeoning Dallas

Innovation District. The Living Lab was the fastest-to-market smart cities initiative in the country

and comprises 10 projects in a six block corridor encompassing five core use cases: resource

efficiency, economic revitalization, public health, mobility, citizen engagement and public safety.

Irving - Artificial Intelligence for Wastewater Infrastructure: Uses artificial intelligence to assess

sewer pipe conditions which helps to prioritize maintenance through predictive assessment,

save staff time, resources and necessary street cuts.

Dallas - Project Safe Neighborhoods Placemaking: The Park at Forest-Audelia is a colorful and vibrant

demonstration park located in North Dallas on a newly purchased piece of Dallas Parks property -

currently a parking lot. Redesigned by the Better Block to improve safety and function, the pop-up

park features sport courts, flex lawn space, shade, seating, a community garden, a play area for kids,

and daily activities ranging from after-school programming to a weekend market featuring global cuisine

and music. Pedestrian crosswalks stretch across the streets while planters and landscaping frame

the space, making the plaza easily accessible and well-defined.

Irving - AMI Network: Implemented Advanced Metering Infrastructure to automate meter reading for

staff efficiencies, enhanced accuracy and customer transparency.

DFW Airport - Apron Lighting: Apron lighting upgraded with wireless control solution has been

deployed for energy savings and more efficient lighting for the safe of ground crews and pilots.

McKinney - "Where Can I Build That?": The code & zoning department built a digital interactive mapping

platform accessible via mobile or desktop to help commercial developers hone in on certain

land use categories prior to engaging staff.

Irving - Rapid Property Assessment App: Assists code compliance in identifying low performing

districts much faster using a mobile app.

Irving - My Government Online: Enhance online permitting process to streamline customer experience

and staff approval process.
